Bio

As a senior data scientist at Global Relay and the founder and research director of DG Sculptor, a nonprofit focused on AI and machine learning research, I have a strong background in the field. Before these roles, I served as the director of research and development at Humanitas Solutions, leading various R&D projects. I received my Ph.D. from McGill University and spent an additional year as a research associate at the Statistical Signal Processing Lab. My research interests currently include advanced AI/ML-based multi-media signal processing, including areas such as speech synthesis and implicit neural representation for AR/VR

About

As a researcher, engineer, and problem-solver, I am skilled in advanced signal processing, machine learning, and various areas of applied mathematics, including matrix analysis, optimization theory, modern optimization, probability, and stochastic processes. I am also an out-of-the-box thinker and a skilled debugger.
However, my greatest strength is my approach to problem-solving. As a fundamentalist scientist, I first understand the problem at hand and then seek out the necessary tools to solve it, rather than relying solely on my skills. I believe in following the scientific process, as advocated by Abraham Maslow's quote: "I suppose it is tempting, if the only tool you have is a hammer, to treat everything as if it were a nail." This approach allows me to be adaptable and efficient in finding solutions to complex problems.
